Providing Better Education

Siani Mobley

School districts aren't providing high quality learning environments for
low income areas. Meaning there's a disinvestment in low quality schools
compared to high quality schools. Which can have significant impacts on
critical outcomes such as student achievement. Large performance gaps
exist between children in the lowest and highest socioeconomic-status.
These performance gaps reflect unmet needs among students who attend
low-income schools. When there isn't a strong support system students
tend to fall behind or rely on negative solutions. Rather than helping the
student accademically teachers enforce consequences which don't solve
the root of the problem. If school districts apply a better support system
academically and emotionally, we can narrow the achievement gap between
low income schools and high income schools.
