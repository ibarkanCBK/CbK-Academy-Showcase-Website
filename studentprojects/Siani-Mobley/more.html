What is the school to prison pipeline?

The school to prison pipeline is used to describe the disproportionate tendency of
children from disadvantaged backgrounds who become incarcerated, due to harsh
punishments. Many students act out when they lack the accademic and emotional
support needed in school. Therefore teachers punish them rather than supporting
them. This system leaves mainly black children susceptible to suspension, detention,
and other punishments that could lead to incarceration. Federal civil rights
investigations have found that black students face harsher consequences compared to
their white peers. Schools with relatively larger minority and poor populations are
more likely to implement criminal justice related policies. If we change our policies
and practices to support, rather than punish and incarcerate children who may be
struggling, we can offer a better future for black children or children from
disadvantaged backgrounds
