The Dangers Of Bullying

About the Problem

Bullying is to seek harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable, it is a problem because when someone is bullied it usually hurts them physically and emotionally. So when someone is getting bullied they could get punched and etc which hurts them physically but when someone hurts them emotionally they usually say some bad things about them and that leads them to do things you never thought they would do ever. If bullying is not stopped a lot of families will be upset because their child did the unthinkable, this is a problem because people become very hurt when they lose someone they love and if they find out who caused their child that pain they will do the unthinkable as well which could cause many people to go to jail.

Key Facts About Bullying

  1. Bullies are often physically bigger or more popular in school than their victims, which gives them their power.
  2. Most bullying occurs in and around school and on playgrounds, although the internet lends itself to particularly distressing forms of bullying.
  3. Most bullies have a downwardly spiraling course through life, as their aggressive behavior interferes with learning, holding a job, and establishing and maintaining intimate relationships.
  4. The best defense against bullying is being socially skilled teaching all children social skills and allowing them to develop confidence in their own abilities.
  5. Labeling an incident as bullying can be important because it influences whether students tell an adult, as well as how adults respond to a student report.
