At-Risk Students

An at-risk student is considered to have a higher probability of failing academically or dropping out of school. A child is considered to be at-risk if they experience:

  • Difficult school life
  • Difficult family life
  • Crime
  • Surrounded by wrong crowd
  • Depression
  • Use of illegal substances

Children from low-income families are more likely to start school with:

  • Limited communication skills
  • Less support from parents with school work
  • More emotional and social problems that interfere with learning

At risk students are more likely to:

  • Engage in sexual activity before 16
  • Become a gang member
  • Get into a fight
  • Steal something worth more than $50
  • Run away from home

The Cycle of Poverty

01

Lack of Education

The cycle of poverty starts with poor education as a result of being an at-risk student without any assistance. They often drop out of school.

02

Lack of Opporunity

With poor education comes little opportunity in the job department.

03

Low Income

With no job opportunities, you make very little income–barely enough to live off of.

04

Poverty

With little money, it is difficult to support your children, who now enter the cycle.

By improving education, we can help those stuck in the cycle break out of it.
