People Affected

Kids From Low-Income Households

Kids from low-income households do not have access to many resources. This makes it harder for them to excell at school and break out of the cycle their family is trapped in.

Kids Who Don't Speak English

Kids who do not speak English as a primary language have difficulty communicating in school, making it harder to succeed.

Kids Who Are Disabled

Kids who are disabled either physically or mentally have a harder time succeeding in school because of their disability.

Education can present opportunities that would have been absent without it. If children struggle in school, or even do not attend school, the statistics show that their income will be lower than if they had gotten a quality education. However, for the children listed above and even many more, school is difficult because there are not adequate resources or measures put into place to make sure they succeed. This starts the cycle of poverty, with their children facing similar issues.
