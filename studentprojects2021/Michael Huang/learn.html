School Budget

In the past decade, there have been multiple decreases in the funding. One of the most prominent examples is in 2011 and 2012 where state and federal funding decreased by 14%. Yet again in 2014, Pennsylvania was forced to shut down 31 schools. This left 4,000 teachers, librarians, counselors, nurses, and support staff without work.

The Disparity Between Neighborhoods

Less wealthy school districts have far less money to spend. On average, the poorest 20% of school districts have $7,866 less per student than the wealthiest 20%. Even within a school district, the amount that a school spends will vary greatly based on what neighborhood it's in. The quality of education for a student can depend so much on where they live.
